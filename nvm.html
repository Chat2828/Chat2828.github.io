Character & Background Art


I made all of the art assets for this indie project. The style our head developer liked was something more scribbly and unpolished, to reflect the story of the game. All of the sprites have alternative facial expressions and blinking animations.

-Home Screen-

The game's opening screen is meant to act as both a hook and a concise representation of the story's premise; it shows the protagonist, Natalie, and the antagonist, Collapsa, who is tormenting her from her mind. The game developer wanted this back-to-back posing in order to represent duality.


-Natalie-

Natalie, the titular character, is designed to represent the playerbase that the game caters towards; teenage girls with more alt tastes. I included the infinity symbol as a nod to the cyclical nature of depression, and the split colors to represent duality. The pink palette is meant to invoke the color of a human brain.


-Bianca-

Bianca, one of the antagonists, was designed to be imposing and very blatantly representing the stereotypical "rich mean girl," particularly in her crisp suit-like outfit. The harsh additions of red are meant to create a sense of danger, particularly with her red gloves that could resemble bloody hands.


-Iris-

Iris serves in the story as Natalie's sense of normalcy and security, representing what she has to lose if she gives into Collapsa's urging. As such, her design is simple and straightforward; a girl you could easily meet in real life. The anchor on her shirt is a very on-the-nose nod to her being Nat's "anchor".


-Collapsa-

Collapsa's design is the most visceral of the main cast; she is the silhouette of a demon's decapitated head residing in a broken mirror. She is both a shadow and a reflection. Additionally, her severed neck adds a necessary touch of gore to the horror concept of the game.


-Cafeteria-

The school cafeteria sets the stage for the game, being its first background. As such, it is the most dreary and simple of all of the environment designs, while also introducing elements that will be common throughout the background arts of the game; the two-toned brick, checkered flooring, and crumpled posters.


-Bathroom-

The bathroom is where the player is introduced to Collapsa, and where Natalie frequently has intense character moments. Here, I add the first blue shades seen in the game, adding more visual complexity. In the center of the frame, often visible in this game style, the suicide hotline number on a poster can be clearly seen in the trash.
