Character Design Examples


-North-

North is a character design that I used as a sort of mannequin while learning various art techniques.

-Inky-

Inky is a monster character- a strange black sludge wearing a human skinsuit as an attempt to blend into society.

-Character Sprite Example -

This character, Bubbles, is a cheerful, feminine slime girl who serves as an intraductory character in her corresponding game.

-Dr. Kelvin Gismond-

This is an example of my character designing process traditionally/on paper. Typically this is the beginning of my process, after numerous warm-up sketches. Shown is a stereotypical mad scientist character reimagined with a more feminine or anime-like body type/clothing style.

-Character Design Ex. -

This is an example of my character designing process digitally, featuring a spider monster character with a spiderling swarm mechanic.

-Helene-

Helene is the protagonist of a long-term project I've been stewing on for the past four or so years, and has gone through many iterations before coming to where she is now. I'll go over her and her lore more in depth in my projects page.

-Vanraj Khan-

Vanraj is my son. I hold him as a character very close to my heart, and I doubt I could fully explain his complexities in this paragraph. That aside, he is most concisely described as a character study on teenaged bullies and the "mean jock" archetype.


Illustration Examples


-Comic Panel Ex. -

This is an example of a more Webtoon-esque art style, with the addition of comic book lettering practice. The shown character is a nurse coming into work for a late night shift.

-Character Portrait -

This is a more painterly character illustration, featuring a fae changeling character, famed for his beauty, named Wellfly. In this piece I also used a much warmer color palette to suit his character and contrast his eyes.

-Character Illustration -

In this piece I attempted to create a more evocative image with symbolism that can be left to the viewer's interpretation. I experimented with the lighting and adding or subtracting details to focus the concept.

-Cartoon Character Ex. -

In this character design, I used a more cartoony rendering and lineart style. I utilized cell shading and vibrant colors to create this effect, along with exaggerated proportions and an unserious character concept; a cow maid character whose horns and hair come together to resemble a mop.
